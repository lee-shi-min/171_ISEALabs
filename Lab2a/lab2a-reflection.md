## Reflection Questions

### 1. Which printer is most cost-effective over 5 years?

The **laser printer** is more cost-effective with a TCO of $7,570 compared to $10,356 for the inkjet. Despite having a higher upfront cost ($150 vs $60), the laser printer saves over $2,700 on consumables (toner is cheaper per page than ink).

### 2. Would the answer change for a home user printing 5 pages per week?

Yes, for a home user printing only 5 pages per week (260 pages per year, 1,300 pages over 5 years):

- **Inkjet:** Would need ~3 ink cartridges ($60) + printer ($60) + paper ($16) = ~$136
- **Laser:** Would need ~1 toner cartridge ($50) + printer ($150) + paper ($16) = ~$216

For low-volume printing, the **inkjet** would be cheaper because the high upfront cost of the laser cannot be justified.

### 3. What other non-financial factors could influence printer selection?

- **Print quality:** Laser is better for text, inkjet for photos
- **Print speed:** Laser is much faster
- **Reliability:** Laser printers have fewer moving parts and last longer
- **Warranty and support**
- **Noise level**
- **Size and weight**
- **Wireless connectivity**

### 4. What cost components are more significant for a large workgroup printer?

- **Toner capacity** (high-yield cartridges reduce replacement frequency)
- **Duty cycle** (monthly page volume capacity)
- **Paper feed reliability** (less jamming)
- **Network management features**
- **Maintenance costs** (replacement parts, service contracts)
- **Energy efficiency** (sleep mode, Eco settings)

### 5. Break-even point

The laser printer starts saving money after approximately **50,000 pages** or **~1.3 years** of printing at 750 pages per week.

## Conclusion

TCO analysis is essential for informed IT purchasing decisions. While inkjet printers have lower upfront costs, laser printers are more economical for high-volume printing. For a home user, an inkjet may suffice, but for an office environment, a laser printer is the better long-term investment.
